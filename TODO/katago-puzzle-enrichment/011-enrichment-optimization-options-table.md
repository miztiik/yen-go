# AI Puzzle Enrichment Pipeline Stages

Before examining optimizations, here is the current logical sequence of the enrichment lab pipeline (`tools/puzzle-enrichment-lab`):

1. **Exploratory Root Scan:** A broad analysis on the root SGF position to generate a pool of candidate moves. KataGo evaluates the standard winrate, policy prior, and score lead.
2. **Per-Candidate Confirmation:** Candidates passing a minimum policy filter (e.g., `>= 0.03`) are individually queried with `confirmation_visits`. Correct Moves (TE) are ranked by highest winrate; Wrong Moves (BM) are ranked by highest policy (finding the "most tempting traps").
3. **Tree Building (Recursive Depth-First Scan):** At **Player Nodes**, the tree follows the best (highest winrate) candidate linearly. At **Opponent Nodes**, the tree branches out (up to `max_branch_width=3`).
4. **Natural Stopping & Edge-Case Detection:** The search halts heuristically based on Level-Aware Depth Profiles (e.g., Novice halts at 2-10 depth, Strong at 4-28). It also halts upon Winrate stability, Ownership convergence, or Seki-like oscillation.
5. **Quality Assessment & SGF Injection:** Generates refutation trees, assigns tags (Ko, Seki, Tesuji), builds AI Correctness levels (`ac:1`, `ac:2`), and injects custom properties (`YM`, `YX`, `YH`).

## Recommendations

1. Execute ENR-01, ENR-03, and ENR-04 first because they are the highest-confidence query-efficiency improvements without requiring quantification in this step.
2. Treat ENR-02 as a phased change: apply Option A immediately, then prepare Option B only after deterministic multi-worker constraints are documented.
3. Use ENR-07 Option B as a guardrail before larger tuning so future changes do not reintroduce duplicated analysis paths.
4. Apply ENR-08 Option B to avoid planning errors caused by mixing superseded ADR text with active runtime defaults.
